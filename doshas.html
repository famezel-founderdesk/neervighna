The Tridosha Theory

Vata, Pitta & Kapha

In Ayurveda, the universe is made of five elements. These combine to form three vital energies known as Doshas. Understanding your dominant dosha is the key to personalized health.

Air & Space

Vata

Vata governs movement in the body, the activities of the nervous system, and the process of elimination. When balanced, Vata promotes creativity and flexibility.

  • Physical: Tall or short, thin frame
  • Energetic: Creative, quick-thinking
  • Imbalance: Anxiety, dryness, insomnia
Fire & Water

Pitta

Pitta governs digestion, metabolism, and energy production. When balanced, Pitta promotes intelligence, courage, and a glowing complexion.

  • Physical: Medium build, warm body
  • Energetic: Sharp, focused, ambitious
  • Imbalance: Anger, inflammation, acid reflux
Earth & Water

Kapha

Kapha governs the structure of the body, lubrication of joints, and stability. When balanced, Kapha expresses love, calmness, and forgiveness.

  • Physical: Solid, heavy build, strong
  • Energetic: Calm, grounded, loyal
  • Imbalance: Weight gain, congestion, lethargy
Signs of Imbalance

Listen to Your Body

Vata Imbalance

  • Anxiety & insomnia
  • Dry skin & hair
  • Constipation & bloating
  • Cold hands and feet

Pitta Imbalance

  • Acid reflux & heartburn
  • Skin rashes & acne
  • Irritability & anger
  • Excessive sweating

Kapha Imbalance

  • Weight gain & lethargy
  • Sinus congestion
  • Depression
  • Slow digestion
Balancing Practices

Restore Harmony

Vata Pacifying Diet

Favor: Warm, cooked foods, root vegetables, healthy oils (ghee, sesame), warming spices (ginger, cinnamon).

Lifestyle: Maintain a regular daily routine. Practice gentle yoga, meditation, and avoid cold environments.

Pitta Pacifying Diet

Favor: Cooling, sweet, and bitter foods. Cucumber, melons, leafy greens, coconut water, and dairy.

Lifestyle: Avoid excessive heat and sun exposure. Engage in calming activities like swimming or walking in nature.

Kapha Pacifying Diet

Favor: Light, warm, and spicy foods. Legumes, honey, pungent spices (black pepper, garlic), and astringent fruits.

Lifestyle: Stay active and engage in vigorous exercise daily. Try new experiences to combat lethargy.
